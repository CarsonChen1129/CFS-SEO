Is High-Yield Checking Right for Me?

A high-yield interest checking account allows you to earn interest on the money in your checking account. CFS offers a variety of interest checking account options, as well as a competitive checking account interest rate. Here you'll find all the interest checking information you need to help you choose the best type of checking account for your needs.

How is my checking account interest rate determined?

Every checking account is different. In most cases, the interest rate on a high-yield checking account is determined by your average daily balance. For example, the higher your average daily balance, the higher your checking account interest rate. This is often presented in tiers as shown in the chart below: insert chart here placeholder Checking account interest rates tend to fluctuate with the economy and are usually lower than money market or CD interest rates. That's the trade-off for giving you liquidity and for your bank not having your funds for a guaranteed period of time. Higher checking account rates are often reserved for loyal bank customers. If you use a lot of bank services, you may want to inquire about these special rates.

Tips for choosing an interest checking account

There are several things to consider when choosing a checking account with interest. The two most common questions are:


  • What is the checking account interest rate(s)
  • What are the minimum opening and average daily balance requirements

Other factors to consider when choosing a high-yield checking account include the monthly service and statement charges, ATM fees, check writing allowances and bounced check fees.

What Are the Advantages of an Online Checking Account?

An online checking account offers a variety of benefits ranging from convenience to security. When you have an online checking account, you can access it anytime, 24/7... CFS offers several different types of checking accounts, all with online banking access. CFS also offers eChecking, an all-electronic online checking account that allows you to earn interest on the funds in your account. You can open an online checking account with a small deposit, and there's no minimum daily balance.

How do I choose the best online checking account?

Assessing your needs is the most important thing to do before choosing any checking account. Determine how many checks you usually write each month, what minimum balance you can comfortably maintain, and what, if any other features, such as interest, online banking and ATM access, are important to you. In most cases, you'll have to deposit your money through the mail with an online checking account. Some banks, like CFS, also allow you to deposit money through an ATM machine or by direct deposit. Since some online banks charge a small fee when you write a paper check, an online checking account may not be the best option if you need to write many checks. These days, however, many bills can be paid online with bill-pay or by debit card, so you may still find an online checking account a reasonable solution.

Is a Free Checking Account Right for Me?

Although free checking accounts don't typically earn interest, they still offer a variety of convenient benefits, ranging from no minimum balances to unlimited transactions.

A free checking account from CFS doesn't require you to maintain a specific minimum daily balance. And you can open a free checking account with a small deposit. Sometimes a free checking account isn't always free. Make sure you read and understand the fine print before opening your free checking account because sometimes there are hidden costs.
